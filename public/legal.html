Privacy Policy

RadioDock (radiodock.app) is operated by the person named above and hosted on GitHub Pages. The application itself does not collect personal data. Your favourites and custom station lists are stored locally in your browser via IndexedDB and never leave your device.

Third-party services accessed when you use the app

  • Radio Browser API (*.api.radio-browser.info) — when you search for stations, your query is sent to this community-run service. Privacy info.
  • RadioDock Metadata Proxy (radiodock-metadata-proxy-1.onrender.com) — when you play a station, the stream URL is sent to this proxy to fetch "now playing" metadata. The proxy is operated by the same person; source code is open at GitHub. The proxy logs only IP / timestamp / request path for operational diagnostics; logs are not retained long-term.
  • Radio stream servers — your browser connects directly to each station's stream server when you press play. We are not the operator of those servers; refer to each station's own privacy notice.
  • GitHub Pages (host of this site) and Render.com (host of the metadata proxy) record standard server logs (IP address, browser type, timestamp). See GitHub's privacy statement and Render's privacy statement.
  • Buy Me a Coffee — only accessed if you click the "Buy me a coffee" link in the footer. See their privacy policy.

Cookies

None. RadioDock sets no HTTP cookies. IndexedDB is used only to store your favourites and custom station lists locally on your device.

Analytics (Umami)

RadioDock uses Umami Cloud (operated by Umami Software, Inc., USA) for privacy-friendly, aggregate usage statistics. Umami does not set cookies, does not store persistent identifiers on your device, does not fingerprint your browser, and does not track you across sites.

What is recorded: pageviews, and a small set of named interaction events (which station was played, which install button was clicked, search filter used, list created/imported/exported/deleted, stream errors). The events do not contain any text you have typed (no search queries, no list names, no station notes).

What Umami briefly processes: your IP address is used on the Umami server to derive a country code and to generate a daily-rotating, hashed session identifier. Neither the IP nor the session hash is retained after the day rolls over, and neither is exposed to the site operator.

Legal basis: Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR — legitimate interest in understanding aggregate product usage to improve the app. Because no information is stored on or read from your terminal, § 25 TTDSG (German implementation of the ePrivacy Directive) does not apply.

How to opt out: use the toggle below, or enable "Do Not Track" in your browser, or use any content blocker. RadioDock works identically with analytics off.

Contracts & further reading: Umami's Data Processing Agreement (Art. 28 GDPR) is published by the provider and forms part of our agreement with them; see also their privacy notice.

Your rights under GDPR / DSGVO
